Privacy Policy

Your data stays yours.

Last updated: April 16, 2026

TL;DR — TAILOR is self-hosted. Your data lives on your machine. We do not run any server, log, or database that receives your content. The Chrome extension sends conversations only to the TAILOR instance you configure — typically your own Mac or Linux box.

1. What TAILOR Is

TAILOR is an open-source memory framework for AI assistants. It runs on your own hardware. There is no cloud service operated by TAILOR or its authors. When you install TAILOR, you become the operator of your own instance.

2. The TAILOR Chrome Extension

The official Chrome extension captures conversations from supported AI chat sites (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) and sends them to your own TAILOR server, whose URL you enter during setup.

What the extension accesses

  • Page content on claude.ai, chatgpt.com, and gemini.google.com — only to read the visible conversation text.
  • Local browser storage — to remember your TAILOR server URL and API token between sessions.

Where the data goes

  • Only to the TAILOR server URL you configured. Nowhere else.
  • No analytics, no telemetry, no third-party services.
  • The extension does not send any data to Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or the extensions authors.
  • If you configure a local URL (e.g. http://localhost:8787), the data never leaves your machine.

What the extension does not do

  • Does not track your browsing outside the supported AI sites.
  • Does not inject any content into the page.
  • Does not sell, share, or aggregate your data — because it does not collect any data centrally.

3. The TAILOR Software (self-hosted)

When you run the TAILOR server on your own machine, all data — your knowledge base, conversations, facts, embeddings — is stored locally in ChromaDB and SQLite files under the TAILOR installation directory. Nothing is transmitted to us or any third party by TAILOR itself.

If you configure external LLM providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google), their respective privacy policies apply to the content you send to them through TAILOR. You control which provider is used and when.

4. Data We Collect

None. TAILOR does not operate any backend service that receives, stores, or processes your data. The authors of TAILOR have no access to your knowledge base, your conversations, or any information processed by your instance.

5. Third-Party Services Used By TAILOR

TAILOR optionally integrates with external services that you enable and configure. When enabled, data is sent directly from your TAILOR instance to those services — not to us.

  • LLM providers — Anthropic, OpenAI, Google (optional; you provide your own API keys).
  • Ollama — runs locally on your machine. No data leaves your hardware.
  • Cloud sync — rclone to OneDrive, Google Drive, Dropbox (optional; credentials managed by you).
  • Email sync — IMAP or Gmail API (optional; credentials managed by you).
  • Telegram — optional bot integration (token managed by you).

For each of these, the respective providers privacy policy applies. The authors of TAILOR do not see or intercept this traffic.

6. The tailormemory.ai Website

This website is a static landing page hosted on Cloudflare Pages. It does not use cookies, analytics, or tracking pixels. Cloudflare collects standard server logs (IP, user agent) for security and abuse prevention, as described in the Cloudflare Privacy Policy.

7. Your Rights

Because TAILOR does not collect or hold your data centrally, there is no data for us to provide, correct, or delete on your behalf. You have complete control over your own instance — export, delete, or backup your knowledge base at any time directly on your hardware.

8. Childrens Privacy

TAILOR is intended for technical users and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children.

9. Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes materially, we will update the "Last updated" date above and note the change in the project repository. Because we do not have user accounts or contact information, we cannot notify users directly — please check this page periodically if the policy matters to you.

10. Contact

For questions about this policy or the TAILOR project, open an issue on the GitHub repository.
